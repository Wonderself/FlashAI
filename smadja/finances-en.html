Finance & EBITDA

The financial case for sovereign AI. Every dollar saved in recurring SaaS costs flows directly to your EBITDA — and, through standard valuation multiples, multiplies into enterprise value.

The Thesis

EBITDA — Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization — measures the pure operating profitability of your business, stripped of financing decisions and accounting choices. It is the metric that investors, acquirers, and lenders use to evaluate your company.

Sovereign AI impacts EBITDA through three levers: reducing recurring SaaS costs by migrating to proprietary infrastructure, automating manual processes to free productive capacity, and unlocking tax incentives applicable to AI-related R&D and innovation expenditure.

At standard valuation multiples (20x to 25x EBITDA), every USD 1,000 of monthly recurring savings generates USD 240,000 to USD 300,000 in enterprise value. This is not theoretical. It is arithmetic.

OPEX to CAPEX: The Accounting Shift

SaaS subscriptions are classified as operating expenses (OPEX) — they erode your EBITDA every month. When you migrate to proprietary infrastructure, the deployment cost is capitalized as an asset (CAPEX) and amortized over its useful life. The recurring SaaS charge disappears from your operating expenses.

Under ASU 2025-06 (effective for fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2025), US GAAP now requires the capitalization of software costs that were previously expensed. This convergence with IFRS treatment reinforces the financial case for sovereign infrastructure: the cost becomes an asset on your balance sheet, not a drag on your income statement.

The net effect: your EBITDA improves immediately upon migration, your balance sheet shows an appreciating technology asset, and your enterprise valuation reflects both improvements through standard multiples.

Tax Incentive Frameworks

Available in both Argentine and French jurisdictions

Argentina

LEC — Knowledge Economy Act

Up to 80% reduction in employer contributions and 60% reduction in corporate income tax for qualifying companies. AI deployment expenditures can strengthen eligibility dossiers.

Argentina

RIMI — Innovation Incentive

Tax credits for R&D and innovation activities. Sovereign AI development qualifies as eligible innovation expenditure under current guidelines.

France

CIR / CII — R&D and Innovation Credits

30% tax credit on R&D expenditure (CIR) and 20% on innovation expenditure (CII, capped at EUR 400K). AI development and deployment costs can qualify under both frameworks.

France

IA Booster / France 2030

Up to 80% subsidized AI diagnostic through Bpifrance programs. Additional financing available through France 2030 for AI industrialization projects.
