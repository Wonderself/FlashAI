Trajectory

Emmanuel Smadja trained in finance before running companies. His academic path began with a BTS in Comptabilité et Gestion — the French higher technical diploma in accounting — and culminated with the DECF (Diplôme d'Études Comptables et Financières), a Bac+4 qualification from ICS Bégué in Paris, one of France's leading schools in accounting and financial education. This foundation is not incidental: it is the reason every conversation with Emmanuel starts with EBITDA, not with technology.

Over twenty years, Emmanuel built, led, and transformed companies across three converging sectors: media, events, and technology. As CEO and executive producer, he managed budgets exceeding EUR 1.5 million, took over performance venues whose revenue he doubled within two months, and conceived international festivals uniting audiences, artists, and sponsors in continental-scale productions. Each of these experiences demanded the same discipline: control costs, maximize returns, and make decisions under pressure with incomplete information.

In parallel, digital consulting brought him to work with companies of every size, from early-stage startups to established organizations seeking transformation. This exposure to diverse business models, cost structures, and corporate cultures is what drives his ability today to rapidly diagnose where artificial intelligence generates real returns — and where it would be a premature investment.

Emmanuel operates through an Israeli Ltd — an agile, operational structure suited to international billing in dollars and euros. His relocation to Buenos Aires, planned for June 2026, will consolidate a presence that is already fully operational via video conference.

The Dual Culture

French methodological rigor and the Israeli innovation ethos are not abstract qualities. They are two concrete operating modes that Emmanuel applies simultaneously to every engagement.

From France, he retains the discipline of accounting, the demand for documentation, adherence to process, and the ability to translate technical complexity into the language of financial leadership. An audit report authored by Emmanuel reads like a board-level document, not a technical specification.

From Israel, he retains the speed of execution, the tolerance for calibrated risk, the capacity to iterate rapidly, and the deep conviction that technological innovation is not a luxury but a competitive necessity. The Israeli ecosystem produces more startups per capita than any other country in the world, and Emmanuel has absorbed for years that culture of pragmatic excellence that characterizes the Startup Nation.

This dual lens enables Emmanuel to evaluate an AI project not solely on its technical viability, but on its impact on EBITDA, its regulatory compliance posture, its tax eligibility, and its contribution to the long-term valuation of the enterprise.

Philosophy

Technological Diplomacy

Technology is a precision instrument, not a blunt force. Every implementation is calibrated to the client's operating context, digital maturity, and absorption capacity. Solutions are not imposed: they are constructed, in dialogue with leadership, as interventions that the business can genuinely integrate and monetize.

Finance First

Every technical decision is evaluated by its impact on EBITDA. If an automation project cannot demonstrate a measurable return within a reasonable horizon, Emmanuel says so and recommends not proceeding. Long-term credibility is worth more than a short-term invoice.

Quiet Excellence

No press releases, no fabricated testimonials, no artificial urgency. Results speak. Clients who stay speak. The rest is noise, and Emmanuel does not participate in it.

Ethics Always

The Human Clause is not a marketing accessory. It is a conviction. Emmanuel proposes to every client a twelve-month commitment of no layoffs linked to automation, accompanied by training for impacted employees. Automation should elevate people, not replace them. When done right, the result is a virtuous cycle: social stability, tax advantages, stronger employer brand, and a more resilient company.
